ODM settles on former MP David Were for vacant Matungu parliamentary seat

March 01, 2024
Post

- Residents of Matungu constituency will go to the by-election on March 4 to elect successor of the late representative Justus Murunga

- The ODM party issued nomination certificate to former area MP David Were with hope to deliver the seat

- The aspirant urged his opponents to embrace peaceful campaigns ahead of the mini polls

Former Matungu MP David Aoko Were has promised to deliver the vacant parliamentary seat back to the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) party.

Residents of Matungu constituency will go to the by-election on March 4 to elect successor of the late representative Justus Murunga.

Speaking at Chungwa House after being handed the party nomination certificate on Wednesday, January 6, Were said he would pick up from where he left in 2017.

The aspirant also used the opportunity to urge his opponents to embrace peaceful campaigns during the mini polls.

"I'm coming back to complete the work I started and that left by Murunga. We will be peaceful, we will not be abusive. In fact, we are begging politicians not to involve in national politics. We want to concentrate on developing Matungu," he stated.

At the same time, the National Election Board chairperson Catherine Mumma confirmed only Were returned his nomination form to beat the December 24 deadline.

Other politicians such as Paul Posho and Jeremiah Namunyu who had also picked the nomination forms did not submit any.

At the same time, the board unveiled three other candidates for by-elections in Kiamokama, London and Huruma wards.

The Mutungu seat fell vacant following Murunga's death.

The former lawmaker died after being rushed to Matungu sub-county hospital where he could not be admitted due to lack of oxygen on November 15.
