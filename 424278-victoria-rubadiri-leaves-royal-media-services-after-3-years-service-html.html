Victoria Rubadiri Heads to BBC London for 3-Month Long Komla Dumor Award Training

July 06, 2024
Post
  • A year after winning the BBC World News Komla Dumor Award, TV presenter Victoria Rubadiri will be heading to London
  • She is expected to undergo a sponsored three-month training that will impart in her essential journalistic skills for both radio, TV, and online media
  • Judges of the award revealed that they found Rubadiri as the candidate of choice due to her passion for sharing African stories

In September 2020, sassy Citizen TV presenter Victoria Rubadiri won the BBC World News Komla Dumor award.

It is an award that exists in honour of Komla Dumor, who worked for the British Broadcasting Corporation but died in 2014 aged 41.

Second winner from Kenya

Komla Dumor was a revered presenter who possessed a rare innate ability to tell stories that resonate with the audience with an unrivalled understanding and simplicity.

"Komla was a well-rounded journalist whose style, though authoritative, was also compassionate, empathetic and uplifting. His ability to give the facts comprehensively, and yet still be attuned to his audience, was something I admired and a skill I seek to emulate," Rubadiri said after being announced winner.

According to the judges, Rubadiri's eloquence and passion for sharing captivating African stories both at her workplace and on social media won their hearts.

Rubadiri is the sixth winner since inception and the second Kenyan to bag the award.

Other winners include Didi Akinyelure, Nancy Kacungira, Solomon Serwanjja, Amina Yuguda, and Kenya's Waihiga Mwaura.

Three-month placement

The mother of one will be flying out to London next week for her three-month placement at the BBC studios.

She is set to attend a training course with the BBC Academy as well as work alongside news teams through which she will acquire valuable journalistic skills and experience.

"The training initially ran for one year but these days it has been compacted into a period of just three months," her director Monicah Kiragu told TUKO.co.ke's Hillary Lisimba.

While cutting the cake in the studio after her Sunday Live bulletin, the vixen quipped about how she was going to miss her guest anchor Jeff Koinange.

Rubadiri holds a Bachelors degree from Temple University in the US and was in the Public Relations space in New Jersey and New York before relocating to Kenya where she quickly became a household name.
