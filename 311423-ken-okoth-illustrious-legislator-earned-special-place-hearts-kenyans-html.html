Ken Okoth: Illustrious legislator who earned himself special place in hearts of Kenyans

March 17, 2024
Post

The cruel hand of death has robbed Kenya of yet another great and visionary leader who earned respect for dedicating his life to serve others.

Kibra MP Ken Okoth died on Friday, July 26, but it is indisputable his legacy as an industrious and illustrious leader who loved the country and his electorates will forever live on.

The humble Kenyan son raised by a single mother in the country's dilapidated slums of Kibera, Okoth's vision and dedication to transform the area into a better environment never died.

He strongly believed in the power of education to transform lives and spur development, even in his days of enduring pain in a hospital bed, Okoth always ensured new schools are build, well equipped and the less privileged got scholarships to continue with studies.

"I’m biased towards the power of education to change people's lives and make society better, because that has been my life story and experience,” he once said during an interview

In a span of seven years, the youthful MP has won the hearts of electorates with mega projects he has launched and managed.

His love for children, women and young people was incomparable and to live his word, he always found time to listen to his people regardless of their background.

His life story was written around education and poor family under the guardianship of a single mother.

His hard work and thirst for education made him receive a scholarship from Save the Children to attend boarding school in Nairobi.

The deceased wanted to attend college after high school, but could not afford money to advance his education in a local university.

He decided to sell newspapers in the morning to fund German language classes in the afternoon as he had hatched a dream to become a certified tour bus driver for German-speaking tourists.

Lady luck knocked and things changed for the best for Okoth when he secured another scholarship to study in New York’s St. Lawrence University.

The former legislator got another chance to study at Georgetown’s BMW Centre for German and European studies but lacked required fees.

The school's dean intervened and awarded him full scholarship.

Upon completing the studies, Okoth got a chance to teach at Virginia’s Potomac School and Swahili culture and language at Georgetown.

In 2007, Okoth became the chairman of the Children of Kibera Foundation where he used to promote vocational training as an educator during his part time.

Okoth also founded the nonprofit organisation in 2007 to assist youth in the Kibera community where he grew up.

The organisation has been instrumental in raising scholarships to liberate students from Nairobi’s broken public.

He was first elected in parliament in 2013 on and Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) and easily retained the seat in 2017 courtesy of his development record.

Even in his pain, Okoth touched hearts of many Kenyans through his stellar and unmatched development record and his departure has broken even more hearts which adored him.
