Kenya Prisons on the Spot for Paying about KSh 1 Billion to Ghost Suppliers

Kenya Prisons has been put on the spot by Auditor-General Nancy Gathungu for paying out KSh 975.7 million to ghost suppliers.

Gathungu said the huge amount was disbursed to unknown companies signalling the money could have been siphoned off from public coffers once again.

"The verification of the payment vouchers at the stations revealed various irregularities as suppliers who were paid at the headquarters were not known to the stations,” Gathungu said as reported by Business Daily.

The auditor-general noted that account books in seven prison stations did not reflect orders or deliveries of various items which included foodstuffs.

“Seven prison stations books do not reflect orders or deliveries of the same and prison stations counter receipt book register do not have series of those counter receipt vouchers (S13) used,” she stated.

In the audit, Gathungu said the records showed KSh 555.6 million was paid at the State Department for Correctional Services on behalf of various prison stations.

Another KSh 419.9 million was overpaid at the stations and did not match what was supplied.

“The actual supplies amounted to KSh 190.3 million and Integrated Financial Management System (IFMIS) payments amounted to KSh 610.3 million," she said.

Parliament had asked Correctional Services Principal Secretary Zeinab Hussein to verify all the pending bills before making any payments.

As of December 2020, the department was set to settle KSh 6.2 billion pending bills.

NYS scandal

The latest corruption scam to rock the Jubilee government is similar to the National Youth Service (NYS) scandal where over KSh 791 million was stolen.

On May 19, Kirinyaga governor who was the Devolution Cabinet Secretary when the funds went missing, appeared in court as a state witness.

The prosecution had asked the court to bar media from covering Waiguru's testimony as a witness, claiming fear of misreporting, a request that the court declined.

The court would hear the graft case on August 5, 6 and September 13, 14 and 16, 2021.
