President John Magufuli expresses shock in Pierre Nkurunziza's death

April 08, 2024
Post

- Nkurunziza died on Tuesday, June 9, after suffering cardiac arrest

- The Burundian leader was set to hand over power in August 2020 after leading the country for 15 years

- Magufuli described him as an astute leader who worked tirelessly to ensure peace, development and democracy became a reality in his country

President John Pombe Magufuli of Tanzania has expressed shock in the sudden demise of outgoing Burundian leader Pierre Nkurunziza.

The government of Burundi announced the death of Nkurunziza on Tuesday, June 9, with reports indicating he suffered a heart attack.

Magufuli hailed the fallen former president as an astute leader who worked tirelessly to ensure peace, development and democracy became a reality in Burundi and the whole of the East African region.

"I am shocked by the death of the outgoing president of Burundi, Pierre Nkurunziza. I will remember him for his steadfast leadership and his efforts to fight for peace, development and democracy. On behalf of the government of Tanzania I would like to pass our sincere condolences to the family and Rwandans at large," he said via his Twitter handle.

Nkurinzaza was hospitalised on Saturday, June 7, after feeling unwell, his condition improved but he suffered a cardiac arrest on Monday leading to his demise.

He was at the helm of Burundi's leadership for 15 years and his regime was marred with political upheavals and tension.

He was regarded as a dictator by critics and human rights groups who constantly protested his iron fist leadership.

In 2015, Nkurinziza plunged the country into war after announcing plans to seek another term in office contrary to the provisions of the constitution.

A coup was staged but failed, hundreds of people died in clashes and dozens fled the country.

Despite the protests, Nkurinziza managed to push for the amendment of the constitution to allow him to seek a third term in office.

He was expected to via in the general election held in May 2020 but opted to step down and was set to hand over power in August.

President Uhuru Kenyatta was among African leaders who sent condolences messages to the family of Nkurunziza and the people of Burundi.

The government of Burundi declared seven days of mourning the fallen leader with flags being flown at half-mast for the same period.
