Breakdown showing how boys performed better than girls in KCSE 2017

June 18, 2024
Post

- A keen breakdown of the just released KCSE results indicate that boys performed better than girls from an overall perspective

- Despite the female students dominating the top slots nationally, boys generally outshone them in by more numbers attaining University-worthy grades

Boys dominated the charts in the just released 2017 KCSE examination results, despite the top student being a girl, TUKO.co.ke has established.

Education Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang’i on Wednesday, December 20 had announced that the ladies had registered better performances than the male students across the six major subjects.

A deeper analysis of the results, however, would reveal otherwise.

According to the full released results, about 70,073 out of the 615, 773 students attained the minimum grade (C+) required to join University.

However, out of the 70,073 students set for tertiary education, boys scooped more slots, with about 41,681 boys attaining C+ and above, which represented 59 percent of the total.

As far as prestigious grades are concerned, 81 male students scored a mean grade of A, compared to female students’ 61.

1,813 males scored A minus whilst about 4,596 recorded a mean grade of B plus. 7,738 managed a B plain and 11, 631 scored a B minus. 14,828 scored a C plus.

In contrast, 901 female students scored an A-, while 2,748 scored a B+, 7344 managed a B plain while females in the B- category were 19385. A further 27860 scored a C+.

From the released results, boys also seemed to have outshone girls in the C plain category, with 21,506 male candidates scoring C plain compared to 18,968 female candidates.

A further breakdown will indicate that the number of A grades across all students increased from 141 to 142,.

Furthermore, 15 counties out of the 47 recorded more female than male candidates.
