William Ruto maintains Uhuru-Raila handshake cannot stop competition for Kibra seat

May 19, 2024
Post

- Ruto said the handshake deal was not meant to scuttle the democratic rights of the two parties

- The deputy president maintained the Kibra contest will not interfere with the cordial relationship between President Uhuru Kenyatta and Raila Odinga

- A section of Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) politicians have been accusing Jubilee leadership of betraying their party leader by fielding a candidate

Deputy President William Ruto has told off those accusing Jubilee leadership of betraying Opposition leader Raila Odinga by endorsing a candidate for the Kibra by-election.

Ruto said the handshake deal between President Uhuru Kenyatta and Raila was not meant to scuttle the democratic rights of the two parties.

In a post he shared on his Twitter handle on Friday, September 20, the seemingly vexed Ruto asked those speculating about end of the handshake to give him a break and focus on other relevant issues.

"The handshake was never meant to kill parties or replace competitive multi party democracy. Those ranting about our party leader endorsing our party candidate should give us a break. So its right for all others to endorse theirs and wrong for Jubilee," he tweeted

The Kibra race got murkier on Wednesday, September 18, after Uhuru publicly endorsed footballer McDonald Mariga to battle out with other candidates contrary to the expectations of Raila's Orange party.

The president's move irked a section of ODM politicians who accused him of going contrary to the spirit of the peace pact he signed with their party leader.

Embakasi East MP Babu Owino and Suba North lawmaker Millie Odhiambo read malice in the manner Jubilee leadership was behaving and warned Raila to be keen lest he risks being shortchanged in the long run.

Uhuru's endorsement of Mariga came as a surprise to many considering the then lingering perception that the former Harambee Stars player was a Ruto project.

While Kibra constituency is predominantly ODM, the recent developments and perceived betrayal games that have played out have complicated the numerical strength of the Opposition outfit, compelling it to work extra hard to cover the required ground.

The party has, however, received support from a section of Jubilee MPs who have publicly denounced Mariga's candidature and warmed up for ODM's Imran Okoth.

Nominated MP Maina Kamanda and his Gatundu South counterpart Moses Kuria have hinted at supporting the ODM nominee at the expense their Jubilee candidate.
