Marianne Kitany's mother recounts she asked Linturi if he loved her daughter or her money

- She revealed the two families enjoyed cordial relationships until late 2016 when her daughter started complaining about the senator's attempt to snatch her property

- The couple is embroiled in a nasty divorce case which has captured the attention of the public thanks to the shocking testimonies from witnesses

The mother of Mithika Linturu's estranged wife, Marianne Kitany, has said she asked the Meru senator whether he genuinely loved her daughter or was only interested in her wealth.

Rodah Chepkoech Kitany who was testifying in the divorce case of the embattled couple said she allowed her daughter to marry Linturi after the politician affirmed his love to her.

Chepkoech who appeared before a Nairobi court on Tuesday, September 24, recounted interrogating the Meru senator during a dowry payment function held at her home in Nandi county and which was witnessed by a section of Kalenjin elders and close relatives from both sides, K24 reported.

"After agreeing on the dowry, Marianne who had been locked in a room was brought before elders and asked whether she loved Linturi and if she really wanted to marry him. She said yes. Linturi was also asked if he loved my daughter or was only interested in her wealth, he affirmed his love to Marianne," she said

The witness who doubles up as a preacher in a Nandi-based church said the event took place in her living room and went ahead to describe how her son-in-law had dressed on that day.

"I even remember the clothes Linturi wore on that day; a checkered shirt and a purple pair of trousers. The dowry was supposed to be paid in the form of animals, but Linturi’s team said it would be difficult to transport animals from Meru to Nandi. So, they gave us cash KSh 100, 000 as dowry," recalled Chepkoech.

The 65-year-old said the two families enjoyed cordial relationships and used to visit each other occasionally until late 2016 when her daughter started complaining about Linturi's attempt to grab her property.

Chepkoech told the court she was saddened to learn that her son-in-law had taken Marianne's title deeds and further attempted to evict her out of her Runda home.

"We later met with Linturi’s people and agreed that he returns the title deeds to Marianne. It was then that my daughter started receiving threats from her husband. The matter is before the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI)," she said.

Her testimony continues to taint the politician's reputation in a messy divorce case that has captured the attention of the public thanks to the shocking revelations and testimonies from witnesses.
