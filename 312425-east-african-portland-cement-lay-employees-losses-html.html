East African Portland Cement to lay off all employees over losses

August 06, 2024
Post

- The company's CEO said the firm has been making losses of KSh 8 million per day

- The company attributed this to high competition and lack of modern equipment in its production process

- The cement producer has over 2,000 employees and the first batch is set to be released in a month's time

Hard economic times have forced companies operating in Kenya to scale down on their human resources in bid to remain afloat and profitable.

The latest company to join the long list of firms that have laid off their employees is East African Portland Cement which has declared it's over 2,000 staff as redundant.

In a memo to employees, the firm’s CEO Stephen Nthei said all positions in the company have been declared redundant and the move will also affect even those in unions.

"Currently we suffer daily losses of up to KSh 8 million. In the last three years, the company’s market share has also dropped drastically impacting negatively on sales,” said Nthei.

The cash strapped company reported a 30% increase in loss to KSh 1.26 billion for the half year ended December 2018 up from the KSh 949.2 million loss it posted six months before.

Nthei said the level of competition from upcoming cement manufactures had seen the firm on the receiving end of losses despite efforts to improve its production.

Other factors for heavy losses included increased output prices, a sluggish market and production challenges arising from the company’s tight working capital position.

To continue with operations, the jobs will be reconfigured and consolidated to a leaner organisational structure.

“As a result of the restructuring program, all positions in the company will be declared redundant and the employees released...The company acknowledges that the exercise is a difficult decision but the best option in the present business circumstances,” he added.

The company is estimated to have over 2,000 employees who are both on contract and permanent pensionable terms.

The first batch of workers is expected to be released within the next one month.

Notably, EAPC is not the first company to lay off its employees given Stanbic Bank is set to fire over 250 employees.

National Bank also sacked 112 workers in June to cut the cost of operation.

Other companies include Telkon Kenya which is set to lay off over 500 employees ahead of its merger with Airtel.
