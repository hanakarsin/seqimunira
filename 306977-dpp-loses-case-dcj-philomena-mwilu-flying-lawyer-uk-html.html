DPP loses case against DCJ Philomena Mwilu despite flying in lawyer from UK

June 30, 2024
Post

- Mwilu was facing 13 counts including fraudulently obtaining money from collapsed Imperial Bank

- The DCJ moved to court to block her prosecution citing illegality of the court process

- Through her lawyers, she petitioned the court to transfer her case to the Judicial Service Commission (JSC)

- The judges noted the prosecuting team illegally obtained Mwilu's bank statements without her prior knowledge

- The government's private prosecutor disclosed he would appeal the ruling

Deputy Chief Justice Philomena Mwilu has been let off the hook by the High Court in Nairobi after her corruption case was thrown out on Friday, May 31.

Mwilu was seeking to block her prosecution in a case she was being accused of abuse of office and economic related crimes as well as improperly obtaining KSh 12 million from the collapsed Imperial Bank.

However, the court found one count on abuse of office and two on fraud were properly before it.

Judges Hellen Omondi, William Musyoka, Mumbi Ngugi, Chacha Mwita and Francis Tuiyot noted the Directorate of Public Prosecution illegally obtained the DCJ's bank statements without her prior knowledge.

The five-judge bench further disagreed with the DPP's government private prosecutor Khawar Qureshi who had requested to have the charge sheet amended afresh.

Qureshi came under fire when the justices accused his client of misinterpreting a court order.

The accused was arrested in August 2018 and arraigned but later on, she petitioned the court to transfer her case so that it could be heard before her employer, the Judicial Service Commission(JSC).

Interestingly, the 61-year-old Supreme Court deputy president is a member of the JSC.

Meanwhile, the prosecuting team intends to appeal the ruling.
