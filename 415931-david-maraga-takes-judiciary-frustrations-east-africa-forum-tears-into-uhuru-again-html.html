Chief justice emeritus David Maraga has once again torn into President Uhuru Kenyatta for failing to appoint six judges as recommended by the Judicial Service Commission (JSC).

Speaking during a virtual meeting at an East African forum, the straight-shooting jurist described how the Judiciary arm of government has been under constant attacks from the Executive.

Maraga criticises Uhuru's decision

Maraga insisted Uhuru's role in the process is ceremonial and as such, he has no choice but to appoint the judges whose names he rejected.

"The appointing authority is the Judicial Service Commission. That authority is, however, begrudged and has been the subject of serious litigation, some of which is ongoing in the appeal process.The courts have consistently held that the president's role to appoint the judges is ceremonial and any refusal to do so within 14 days is unconstitutional," Maraga said as reported by NTV Kenya.

Former JSC vice-chair Mercy Deche echoes Maraga

Former JSC vice-chair Mercy Deche who played a role in the nomination of the judges echoed Maraga's sentiments.

"The matter cannot be back to the JSC. The president must do the right thing," she said.

The discussion was attended by judicial representatives from other East African states and focused on gains eroded in judicial independence in the region.

Maraga calls for Uhuru's impeachment

In his first statement after Uhuru appointed 34 out of 40 judges, Maraga had called on parliament to impeach the president for failing to protect the constitution according to his oath of office.

Speaking to KTN News on Wednesday, June 9, Maraga said the only way to restore order was to send the head of state home.

"We risk descending into a banana republic. The only thing left is for parliament to impeach President Uhuru Kenyatta. It is the duty of parliament when the president has violated the constitution to take steps to remove him. That's the only way you can have order. If this kind of act is allowed to continue, we are descending into a banana republic very easily," Maraga said.

High court bars JSC from acting on Uhuru's report

High Court Judge James Makaua had barred the JSC from acting on Uhuru's alleged integrity report on the six rejected judges.
