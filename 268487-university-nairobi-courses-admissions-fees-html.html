University of Nairobi: courses, admissions and fees

May 10, 2024
Post

The University of Nairobi founded under cap 210 Act of Parliament is the forerunner institution of university education in Kenya and its environs. Over time, this institution has diversified its academic programmes to include various specializations in humanities, applied science, sciences, social sciences, arts, and technology. The University of Nairobi offers approximately over two hundred courses presently.

Apart from being a world-class university, there are several other reasons to pursue a course at the University of Nairobi. University of Nairobi admissions

The University of Nairobi admissions offers three academic options. University of Nairobi undergraduate courses are of two categories; Module I and Module II. Undergraduate Module I refer to the regular programme for students sponsored by the Kenyan government. The admissions for this category are made holistically through Kenya Universities and Colleges Central Placement Service (KUCCPS) past National Joint Admission Board (JAB).

Second is the Undergraduate Module II for the self-sponsored students for those that failed to meet KUCCPS sponsorship. Admissions for these programmes are conducted internally at the University of Nairobi by obtaining an application form from UNES offices after paying the application fee. Lastly, we have the postgraduate programme whose admissions are done by the Board of Postgraduate Studies (BPS).

University of Nairobi Course Requirements

There are varying minimum admission prerequisites for different courses at Nairobi University.

  • Bridging Programmes

To pursue any bridging course, one must have a KCSE mean grade C+ or an equivalent or at least a C- in the subject they wish to bridge.

  • Certificate Programmes

For certificate courses, the student must have a KCSE mean grade of C- or equivalent an O level mean score of Division III or equivalent and an A Level qualification with a minimum of two subsidiary passes or equivalent.

  • Diploma Courses

Diploma applicants must have a KCSE mean grade of C- in addition to a certificate or can have O and A level qualification similar to those of the certificate programme. The certification needs to be from a registered university recognized by the University of Nairobi Senate. Moreover, the certification must have taken at least one semester.

  • Undergraduate Programmes

For the admission to any undergraduate course, there are various prerequisites as outlined below. One can have any one of these minimum qualifications.

  • KCSE C+ Mean grade or
  • a KCSE Mean Grade C- plus a certificate and a Diploma or
  • level Division III plus a certificate and Diploma or an O level Division III plus a diploma or
  • A diploma from any recognized University and an aggregate of C in KCSE or equivalent, CPAII/CPSII/CIPS or equivalent and should have a mean of C in KCSE or
  • level Division III or Higher National Diploma in the related field and a KCSE C mean grade or level candidates with two first passes or equivalent ion the related fields or a degree from any authorized university.
