Sharon and Melon: Kakamega Identical Twin Girls Score C Plus, C Plain

March 01, 2024
Post

- Sharon, Melon and Mevis were to sit the Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education exams in 2019

- However, the three opted out of the exams despite having spent six months in the fourth form

- Richard Olukhakha confirmed Sharon and Melon, who now stay together, finally sat the 2020 KCSE and posted impressive results

The famous identical Kakamega twin girls who were believed to have been separated from each other at birth in 1999 were among the candidates in the 2020 KCSE exams.

This was after Sharon Matias, Melon Lutenyo, and Mevis opted out of the 2019 KCSE so that they could sit exams in 2020 owing to the disruptions they had gone through while waiting for the DNA results.

At that time, Melon and Sharon were Form Four candidates at Kongoni Secondary School and Shikoti Girls High School, respectively, in Kakamega county.

Mevis, on the other hand, was a student at Kimsong Girls High School in Trans-Nzoia county.

The three, later on, changed schools; Sharon joined Butere Girls' High School, Melvis proceeded to St. Mary's Mumias and Melon settled for Bishop Sulumeti Girls' all in Kakamega county.

When TUKO.co.ke contacted Sharon and her father, Richard Olukhakha, they talked about his daily business in Kakamega. Melon was not at home.

Olukhakha confirmed Sharon and Melon, who now stay together, enrolled for the 2020 KCSE and posted impressive results.

According to Olukhakha, Sharon, a Butere Girls' High School candidate, managed a C plus while her sister Melon had a C plain.

"Indeed the girls performed well, beyond our expectation as a family. Sharon got a C plus. Melon did not disappoint as she managed C plain," he told TUKO.co.ke.

Without hesitation, Sharon disclosed she had 51 points but was quick to mention that that Melon was not at home and was not comfortable sharing her results without her consent.

"I'm sorry but I'm a bit tensed because I didn't expect that performance,.....my sister is out for some nature walk," she responded before confirming Melon scored 39 points.

The father is now asking for financial support to cater to the girls' school fees.

"I want them to join universities at the same time. But this can only be achieved if I had money. There are people who promised to pay their fees up to university level but they never fulfilled the promises," he added.

The reunion

The three girls hit the headline in 2019 after Sharon and Melon connected via Facebook, and to them, it was like looking at a mirror.
