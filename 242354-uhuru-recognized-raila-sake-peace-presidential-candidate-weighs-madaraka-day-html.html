Uhuru should have recognized Raila for the sake of peace former Presidential candidate weighs in

March 06, 2024
Post

- The Madaraka day celebrations in Nyeri was marked by one unfortunate highlight

- Opposition leader Raila Odinga was never given a platform to address Kenyans during the ceremony, despite being in the presence of President Uhuru Kenyatta

- This sparked an outrage all over, with Kenyans going for the President

- One person, who has in the past battled for Presidency with Uhuru and Raila, has also weighed in on the issue

Former Presidential aspirant James Ole Kiyapi has joined in the raging debate on whether it was right for Uhuru Kenyatta to completely disregard opposition leader Raila Odinga during the Madaraka day celebrations.

Raila impressed, surprised and caused a little suspicion all at the same time by attending the national ceremony which was being held at Uhuru Kenyatta’s stronghold in Nyeri county.

As TUKO.co.ke reported earlier did not go opposition’s way as the ODM leader barely got a chance to address the nation, neither did he get a mention from the President – a move which sparked a fire on social media.

Speaking on the issue, Ole Kiyapi faulted the President, adding saying that in the spirit of the day, he should have at least given Raila some form of acknowledgement.

“President Uhuru Kenyatta and his Deputy should have in the spirit of the day recognized the Presence of opposition leader Raila Odinga!” He screamed via twitter.

Majority of Kenyans who were sharing the same sentiments also chimed in Kiyapi’s tweet with some harsh statements.

At the same time, ODM officials based in the Jubilee stronghold also faulted the government for failing to recognise their leader Raila Odinga.

NASA coordinator in the Mount Kenya region Gitonga Wathanga expressed his shock in the fact that Raila was treated like a regular during the landmark celebrations.

"A large part of celebration, including entertainment, centred around an audience from the Kikuyu community," he lamented.
