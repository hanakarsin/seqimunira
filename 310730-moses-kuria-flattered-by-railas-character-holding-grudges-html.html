Moses Kuria flattered by Raila's character of never holding grudges

August 01, 2024
Post

Gatundu South MP Moses Kuria has hailed Opposition leader Raila Odinga for playing mature politics free of grudges.

Kuria who was Raila's fiercest critic in the run up to 2017 General Election said he has come to realise the Opposition supremo was an amazing man.

The lawmaker who made the confession during an interview with KTN News on Wednesday, July 17, went ahead to state he would like to be like Raila when he "grows up" .

"My relationship with Odinga is very good. He’s an amazing man who does not hold grudges. I want to be like him when I grow up," he said

The revelation comes as a surprise to many considering his verbal rants directed at the ODM leader in the last election.

Kuria who is known for spewing venom, at one point called for a manhunt on Raila supporters who chose to vote for him in the 2017 repeat presidential election which the former premier boycotted.

He went further to threaten to put the Opposition leader under house arrest if he attempted to interfere with the repeat election.

"If Raila tries to stop us from voting on October 17, 2017, we will place him under house arrest just the way we did to Jaramogi,” he wrote on his Facebook page then

In 2015, Kuria blamed the terror attack on Gikomba market to Raila's Luo community and he was arrested and charged for hate speech.

Prior to that, the Gatundu South MP had in July 2014 equally linked the ODM leader's team to Somalia-based terror group al-Shabaab.

He made the allegation after the militia group attacked Hindi and Gamba villages in Lamu and Tana River counties respectively.

The attack that erupted on the night of Saturday, July 2014 left more than 20 people dead with many others injured.

The politician made more other veiled attacks against Raila including publicly insulting his community over objection to circumcision.

The two leaders later had lunch together and the MP promised to end the onslaught on the former premier.

But the promise was, however, short lived as Kuria continued with his tongue lashing later on.

He only toned down his scathing public attacks after the ODM leader and President Uhuru Kenyatta agreed to work together following the famous March 9, 2018 handshake.

Since then, the lawmaker has been in good terms with Raila and his supporters, and was recently warmly welcomed in ODM leader's home turf city of Kisumu.
