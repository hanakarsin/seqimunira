Kenyans on social media have raised concerns about Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) party leader Raila Odinga's health after he confused ex-Kibwezi lawmaker Kalembe Ndile with Wiper leader Kalonzo Musyoka during the former's burial in Makueni.

While eulogising the late Ndile, the former prime minister was heard referring to him as Kalonzo on two occasions prompting mourners to correct him.

"Kalonzo was someone who was a development-oriented leader...sorry Kalembe...I have come here to offer a message of condolence from Ugandan president Yoweri Kaguta Museveni. Museveni knew Kalonzo very well in my hometown of Bondo...sorry Kalembe," Raila said amid murmurs.

Kenyans concerned

His video went viral and Kenyans on Twitter wondered if he was fine.

@DaisyKenyan noted:

"Maybe it's time Raila Odinga should retire. Kalembe isn't close to Kalonzo."

@eastleighpundit posed:

"Is Raila Odinga okay?"

Andrew commented:

"He is not okay my friend...on several occasions lately, he's becoming exactly like HE Mwai Kibaki."

Dennis Mokaya opined:

" Baba is tired and won't have the energy to push through the vigorous campaigns."

Kevin Anyango wrote:

"He had COVID-19. Science says there could be long side effects! It’s never normal to confuse a name of a dead person with the living. This isn’t a joke!"

@olegunersoldier differed:

"I don't see anything odd here, even a young man can confuse similar names, Baba is very sharp and okay, based on his speech during Madaraka Day."

Ndile breathed his last on Sunday, May 30.

Confirming his demise, the former lawmaker's family said the 57-year-old succumbed after struggling with a long illness.

Raila's hoarse voice fails him in Meru

In February 2020, Raila was forced to deliver a short speech at the Meru Building Bridges Initiative rally because of a hoarse voice.

The veteran politician, a chief proponent of BBI, strained to address the large crowd that had gathered at Kinoru Stadium, Meru, as his voice kept fading away.

"Nashindwa na sauti... lakini jana nilikuja hapa nikalala hapa..nilikuja na vijana wakaniambia baba hapa Meru nobody can stop reggae...( I have lost my voice ... but yesterday I came here and slept here..I came with young people who assured me BBI reggae cannot be stopped in Meru)," he said.
