Hershel Williams: US naval ship enters Mombasa for first time in over a decade

May 03, 2024
Post

- Hershel “Woody” Williams is a gigantic water vessel with great military capabilities manned by the US Navy

- It was deployed for its mission to Africa in July 2020 and its docking in Kenya makes it the first U.S. naval ship to enter the port of Mombasa in over a decade

- Hershel “Woody” Williams has capabilities such as aviation facilities, berthing, equipment staging and command and control assets

- Kenyan troops and military officers and troops will tour the ship to learn more about it but due to COVID-19 restrictions, they will be limited to open spaces of the vessel

US Navy's expeditionary Sea Base USS Hershel “Woody” Williams has docked at the port of Mombasa in support of its 2021 African coast deployment.

The vessel, manned by the US Navy, arrived in Mombasa on Monday, February 8, became the first U.S. naval ship to enter the port of Mombasa in over a decade.

Hershel Williams left Naval Station Norkfolk, based in Virginia for Africa in late July 2020.

The ship, which has great military and navigation capabilities, measures about 238 meters long and is manned by a team of a hybrid crew of 100 sailors and 44 civilian mariners.

Tour to vessel

Kenyan troops are expected to visit Hershel Williams at the harbour where apart from training, they will learn more about capabilities of the gigantic military vessel.

"The visit of the USS Hershel “Woody” Williams is a mark of the deep and enduring relationship between the United States and Kenya,” said Eric Kneedler, Chargé d’Affaires a.i., at the U.S. Embassy Nairobi.

Due to COVID-19 restrictions, participants, (those who will be touring the vessel) will be limited to open space areas of the ship.

The port visit facilitates engagement opportunities between Kenyan officials and military leadership.

US-Kenya military relations

Since 1963, the US has been a strategic partner of Kenya and among goodies, Nairobi has benefited from Washington is an upgrade of its military equipment and capacity building of troops.

USS Hershel “Woody” Williams is currently deployed to the eastern coast of Africa to demonstrate their growth in strategic partnerships and U.S. commitment to African countries through interoperability training, maritime security and safety to allow for freedom of navigation in the region.

Capabilities of USS Hershel “Woody” Williams

USS Hershel “Woody” Williams has four core capabilities which include aviation facilities, berthing, equipment staging and command and control assets.

The ship also has the capability of conducting an attack but mostly if it's defending itself from an enemy.
