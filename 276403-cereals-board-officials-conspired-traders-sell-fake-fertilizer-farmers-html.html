Cereals board officials conspired with traders to sell fake fertilizer to farmers

February 17, 2024
Post

- The adulterated fertilizer was sold to unsuspecting traders at KSh 3,500 per 50 kG bag

- The traders allegedly received subsidized fertilizer from NCPB and repackaged before selling

- The fertilizer was to be sold at KSh 1,500 per bag, which means the traders made profit of KSh 2000 per bag

- NCPB is also involved in a separate graft scandal in which eight traders were allegedly paid KSh 2 billion

The National Cereals and Produce Board (NCPB) has been hit with yet another graft scandal involving KSh 2.5 billion that had been allocated for importation of fertilizer in 2014.

Corrupt NCPB officials conspired with unscrupulous traders to plunder the money at the expense of the poor local farmers.

Appearing before the National Assembly’s Public Accounts Committee on Friday, June 8, Agriculture Principal Secretary Richard Lesiyampe revealed there were issues with the distribution of the subsidized fertilizer.

The PS said unscrupulous traders purchased the fertilizer from NCPB at lower price, repackaged it in foreign bags then sold it.

Lesiyampe claimed some of the traders mixed the fertilizer with grass and rocks before selling it to the unsuspecting farmers.

The adulterated subsidized fertilizer was sold at KSh 3,500 per 50 Kg bag, even though it was supposed to be sold directly to the farmers at KSh 1,500 per 50 Kg bag according to Lesiyampe.

This means the traders made a profit of KSh 2000 per bag.

Lesiyampe said a number of suspects had been arrested in connection with the multi-billion fertilizer scam.

TUKO.co.ke understands investigation is ongoing.

Authorities are also investigating a separate scam in which NCPB was accused of paying KSh 2 billion to eight traders to supply cheaply imported maize.

Detectives discovered senior NCPB officials conspired with traders to stock up six depots with imported maize and profiteered from the illegal trade at the expense of the local farmers.

Several NCPB employees were on May 21, 2018, sent on compulsory leave as probe into the costly maize scam at the cereals board kicked off.

Among those suspended by Lesiyampe was the boad's CEO Newton Terer who was replaced by Albin Sang.
