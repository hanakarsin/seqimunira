A hilarious video of Ainabkoi Member of Parliament William Chepkut calling the late Kiambaa MP, Paul Koinange, on the loudspeaker to seek help has reemerged hours after the latter's death.

In the clip, the humorous lawmaker was heard pleading with Koinange to assist in investigations after Ibrahim Murgor, the son of the late retired president Daniel Moi's ex-presidential escort commander Charles Kimurgor, was attacked and killed by unknown people in Ainabkoi.

Koinange, at the time, served as the chairperson of the Departmental Committee on Administration and National Security.

"Hello chairman, I want to inform you of some sad news that has occurred in my constituency. Mr Koinange, sir, the son of the late retired president Daniel Moi's ex-presidential escort commander Charles Kimurgor, Ibrahim, was killed, and I want to convey heartful sorrow."I want you to move with speed and ask Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) boss George Kinoti to take action. Please this case over because you are my chairman. My people are crying and are calling for the reshuffle of DCI officers in this region," Chepkut said.

During the call, Chepkut praised Koinange as a great leader and pledged his loyalty to him even as the Kiambaa MP promised to follow up on the case to ensure justice.

"Thank you for taking time out of your busy schedule to pick my phone call. Indeed you are a great chairman in this republic, and you are born again. You are leading us well in the National Assembly. Thank you," the Ainabkoi lawmaker added.

Koinange's death

Koinange succumbed to COVID-19 complications on Wednesday, March 31, at Nairobi Hospital according to his family.

He was elected on a Jubilee Party ticket in the 2017 General Election.

President Uhuru Kenyatta mourned the lawmaker as a dependable leader who was a keen and steadfast supporter of Kenya's peace agenda as the National Security Committee's chairperson.
