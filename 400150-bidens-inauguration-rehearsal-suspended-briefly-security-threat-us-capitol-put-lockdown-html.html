Biden's inauguration rehearsal suspended briefly over security threat as US Capitol put on lockdown

March 20, 2024
Post

- The US Capitol was on Monday put on lockdown due to 'security threat' ahead of Joe Biden's inauguration

- Biden would be sworn-in as the new president of America tomorrow, Wednesday, January 20

- More security personnel have been deployed to Washington DC ahead of the important event

The US Capitol building in Washington DC was on Monday, January 18, put on lockdown owing to what authorities termed as a security threat.

Armed troops from the National Guard were deployed on the streets to beef-up security ahead of Joe Biden's inauguration slated for Wednesday, January 20.

TUKO.co.ke understands that presence of security personnel was increased in Washington DC to avert any form of unrest, especially after the January 6 attack on the premise by supporters of outgoing US president Donald Trump.

BBC reported that the rehearsal for Biden's inauguration was discontinued briefly on Monday after smoke was seen near the Capitol. Firefighters also responded to a fire nearby and extinguished it.

An alert circulated to Capitol staff said the complex was on lockdown, with no one being allowed in or out.

All 50 US states and the District of Columbia are on alert for possible violent protests, ahead of Biden's inauguration and FBI has warned of possible armed marches by pro-Trump supporters.

Meanwhile, outgoing US first lady Melania Trump in a recorded video on Monday condemned violence stating it was "never the answer and it will never be justified".

"In all circumstances, I ask all Americans to be ambassadors of the best. To focus on what unites us...to rise above what divides us...to always choose love over hatred and peace over violence. No words can express the gratitude I have, having served as your first lady. To the people of this country, you will be in my heart forever," Melania said.
